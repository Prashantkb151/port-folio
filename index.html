Senior Research Fellow
  • Performed experimental studies focussing on DC discharge systems with different plasma boundary walls. These studies include the transition of anode sheath from an unconventional ion sheath to a conventional electron-rich sheath while plasma boundary changes from an insulating to a conducting boundary. A negative differential resistance (NDR) has been observed in V–I characteristics as an effect of mixed plasma boundaries (insulating and conducting), designed in such a way that discharge can flow from an insulating to a conducting boundary. In addition, a novel and very interesting feature of anode glow, i.e., rotating multiple plasma blobs close to the anode has been reported with different types of oscillations associated with it. This has been observed only for a specific regime of pressure and current when the electrode system is highly asymmetric.
